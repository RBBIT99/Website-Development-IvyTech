Scene Organization

Time, Place, Person, Action. These are the four staples of scene organization. When you're writing a paragraph you may find yourself with this huge blob of text that may be dizzying for some people to read, or make it hard to focus on the words. This is why paragraphs are a thing. No one wants to read a page with no breaks, no sectioning. But how do you create that effect in a story?

Simple. Time, Place, Person, Action. When time passes, make the paragraph, when the place and scene changes, make a new paragraph when a new person speaks, write a new paragraph, when someone does a new action, make a new paragraph. This separates and sections your page in a way that makes it easier on readers to keep up with, and understand. Making the page feel spaced out and longer instead of just having it all smooshed together.
