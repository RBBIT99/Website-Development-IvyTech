The Dreaded Curse of Said. Some alternatives!

The dreaded curse of said. It's about as mentioned as 'show not tell'! Which, in it's own way is wrong. The curse of said doesn't exist. It's completely fine to use 'he said', and 'she says', but where authors and writers are correct is in the fact that it should be used as sparingly as all the rest! 'Said' is fine, but as with all things temperance is key. Never too little, never too much. Thus, this is a list of alternatives you can use instead!

Said(neutral) Spoke
Good alternative, but gets dull quickly.		 Mused
Good when the character is thoughtful, or thinking aloud. 'Thought aloud' is a good alternative.		 Crooned
To speak in a gentle voice. Good for when you want a character to sound like Duchess from the Aristocats, or like a mother doting on her kid.		 Finished
Typically used at the end of an explanation, or to 'end' the conversation.		 Mentioned
Used very often, and really needs no explanation. To briefly speak about something.
Said(Anger) Accused
Self explanatory, good for when a character is frustrated with a situation and needs someone to point a finger to.		 Spat
Another commonly used word for someone who is angry, usually used as a quick retort.		 Rant
It's really just angry chatter and jabber. There's no real point or theme, and that's the point itself. It's aimless, it's illogical. Just as people are.		 Chastised
There's a lot of synonyms for this that really mean the same thing, like chided or condemned. Acused can also be used for this.		 Hissed
Hissed is like spat in it's mannerisms. It's a short quick shot of anger that's biting. But hissed implies it's much quieter, like you want to be angry but shouting is going to wake the whole house.
Said(Happiness) Squealed
Shrill noise, loud cry. A sentence that within context just screams 'this person just saw the greatest thing in their life' 		Joked
Another common phrase used a lot, because it's just self explanatory. Something said in jest, not to be taken seriously.		 Rant
It's really just angry chatter and jabber. There's no real point or theme, and that's the point itself. It's aimless, it's illogical. Just as people are.		 Sang
It's really only used for the kinda happy-go-lucky optimistic kind of characters. Unless they're ACTUALLY singing a song, you probably won't use this for anyone else.		 Cheered
This is again self explanatory, but makes much more sense within a context. This is triumphant, they won something, or solved something. They have a good reason to make some noise.

Of course, if I was going to lay out all the alternatives for you, this page alone would be a digital mile long. But I do have an alternative.
