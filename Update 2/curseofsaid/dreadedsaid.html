The Dreaded Curse of Said. Some alternatives!

The dreaded curse of said. It's about as mentioned as 'show not tell'! Which, in it's own way is wrong. The curse of said doesn't exist. It's completely fine to use 'he said', and 'she says', but where authors and writers are correct is in the fact that it should be used as sparingly as all the rest! 'Said' is fine, but as with all things temperance is key. Never too little, never too much. Thus, this is a list of alternatives you can use instead!

Said(neutral) Spoke. Good alternative, but gets dull quickly. Mused. Good when the character is thoughtful, or thinking aloud. 'Thought aloud' is a good alternative. Crooned. To speak in a gentle voice. Good for when you want a character to sound like Duchess from the Aristocats, or like a mother doting on her kid. Finished. Typically used at the end of an explanation, or to 'end' the conversation. Mentioned. Used very often, and really needs no explanation. To briefly speak about something.

As you might notice, a lot of these may be familiar. I did go for uncommon words, but when speaking neutrally really any 'neutral' way of talking can be a common word. As long as it contributes to the character's current action or thoughts and feelings, as even when speaking neutrally you are still speaking with a feeling.

Said(Anger) Accused. Self explanatory, good for when a character is frustrated with a situation and needs someone to point a finger to. Spat. Another commonly used word for someone who is angry, usually used as a quick retort. Rant. It's really just angry chatter and jabber. There's no real point or theme, and that's the point itself. It's aimless, it's illogical. Just as people are. Chastised. There's a lot of synonyms for this that really mean the same thing, like chided or condemned. Acused can also be used for this. Hissed. Hissed is like spat in it's mannerisms. It's a short quick shot of anger that's biting. But hissed implies it's much quieter, like you want to be angry but shouting is going to wake the whole house.

Unlike with a neutral stance, anger isn't something you can really use 'said' in. Anger is personal, anger is loud. Anger is capslock or bold or italics. It's emphasis, and 'said' carries no emphasis.

Said(Happiness) Squealed. Shrill noise, loud cry. A sentence that within context just screams 'this person just saw the greatest thing in their life' Joked. Another common phrase used a lot, because it's just self explanatory. Something said in jest, not to be taken seriously. Rant. It's really just angry chatter and jabber. There's no real point or theme, and that's the point itself. It's aimless, it's illogical. Just as people are. Sang. It's really only used for the kinda happy-go-lucky optimistic kind of characters. Unless they're ACTUALLY singing a song, you probably won't use this for anyone else. Cheered. This is again self explanatory, but makes much more sense within a context. This is triumphant, they won something, or solved something. They have a good reason to make some noise.

Happiness is in an odd state. Writing this section made me feel odd because the words FELT odd and out of place. I felt like my mind was telling me 'but I wouldn't use this in a sentence' and it's right. When it comes to a character 'saying something happily' it's usually just that. It's something neutral with an adjective. "She said with a smile", and the likes.

Of course, if I was going to lay out all the alternatives for you, this page alone would be a digital mile long. But I do have an alternative.
